CLINT VONK  (Iowa Fastpitch Hall Of Fame 2004 Inductee)

 

 

, ,

1965 - Started playing fastpitch softball with Liz's Tap at age 18

 

1966 - Played for Starr Reality. Placed 2nd in the City. Was voted most valuable player in the Perry Invitational tournament.

 

1967 - Played for Metro Reality. Finish 2nd in the City.

 

1968 (DM Merchants) thru 1978 - Played 2nd base for Reames Frozen Noodles.

Winning numerous City/Metro and Sub State titles.

 

1974 - Voted most valuable player for Reames Frozen Noodles with only 7 strikeouts in 235 plate appearances.

 

1975 - Reames Noodles won the City Championship.

 

1978 - Reames Noodles again won the City Championship.

. Clint played with Reames Noodles the majority of his career and this team won numerous titles and

tournament championships such as the Prairie Du Chien Invitational.

. Batted "lead off' and second with a life time .328 batting average against some of the best pitching in the country. '"

. Was known as a "hard out" and a hitter who would move the ball by the other teams in the state.

. A great "bat handler" with base stealing ability.

. A great team player
